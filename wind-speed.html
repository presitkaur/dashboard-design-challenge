Analysis

For some cities, wind speed appears to increase the further away a city is from the equator. However for the most part, cities- regardless of their latitude- had a generally low wind speed. The data displayed here cannot conclusively prove that latitude influences the wind speed in a particular city.

Wind is caused by the differences in atmospheric and tempertaures of a particular area. As cool air sinks towards the surface, warm air rises- generating wind. Geographic features such as mountains or deserts are some of the factors that can influence wind speed. There are several lurking variables within this analysis that can further be explored with other datasets.
